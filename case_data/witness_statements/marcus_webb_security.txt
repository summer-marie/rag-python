WITNESS STATEMENT — MARCUS WEBB
Date of Interview: Morning after incident
Interviewing Officer: Detective Lara Hoang
Relation to Victim: Night security guard at Ashworth PR building (no direct relationship to victim)

---

I work the overnight security desk at the Caldwell Street building. Ashworth PR is one of the tenants on floors four and five.

At approximately 7:30 PM, I observed Mr. Oliver Ashworth exit through the main lobby. I'm certain about the time because I had just logged my 7:30 desk check. He was wearing a coat and had a burgundy scarf around his neck. He was talking on his phone. His voice was elevated — I wouldn't say yelling, but it wasn't a normal indoor-voice conversation. I heard him say something like "she's not going to do this" before the door closed behind him.

I did not see him again until 11:05 PM. That's confirmed by the keycard entry log. He said he forgot a folder with some contracts in it. He was only upstairs for about twelve minutes and came back down with a manila folder. He seemed — I don't know — composed. Like, maybe too calm for that hour.

Here is where it gets confusing for me: I was at my desk from 7:30 PM onward without a break until 11:05 PM when Mr. Ashworth returned. I did not leave the lobby. But I later found out that another person used the tenant keycard access on the stairwell door to the fourth floor at 10:15 PM — it's in the building log. That access card was registered to Mr. Ashworth. He told me he was not in the building between 7:30 and 11:05. But the stairwell log says differently.

I'm just telling you what I saw and what the logs say. I don't want any trouble.

— Marcus Webb

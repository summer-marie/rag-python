WITNESS STATEMENT — THOMAS HALE
Date of Interview: Morning after incident
Interviewing Officer: Detective Lara Hoang
Relation to Victim: None (witness by proximity)

---

I run the Harwick Bridge loop every night, rain or shine. I've been doing it for three years. I crossed the bridge heading south at around 10:30 PM, maybe a minute or two after — I know because I check my running app when I hit the bridge midpoint.

There was a woman standing at the east railing. She was alone. She was wearing a long, dark green wool coat — I noticed because it was a cold night and she was just standing there, not moving, staring down at her phone. She wasn't dressed for the weather. No hat, no gloves. I thought maybe she was waiting for someone or taking a photo for social media or something. People do that on the bridge at night because of the lights.

I did not stop. I nodded as I passed and she didn't look up. I can't say for certain what her face looked like but her build and height matched the photos I was later shown.

Here's the thing that sticks with me: she was completely alone. There was no one else on the bridge when I crossed. I came from the north approach, jogged the full length, and exited the south approach. I did not see another person anywhere on the bridge or either approach path.

I did not see anyone in a burgundy scarf. I did not see anyone near the east bench. I did not hear any argument or raised voices. The bridge was quiet.

I wish I had stopped. I really do.

— Thomas Hale

WITNESS STATEMENT — PATRICE OKONKWO
Date of Interview: Morning after incident
Interviewing Officer: Detective Lara Hoang
Relation to Victim: Bartender at The Lantern Bar, regular server to Vivienne Ashworth

---

I knew Vivienne. She came in maybe once or twice a week, always sat in the booths near the back. She was a regular.

On the night in question, she arrived around 8:00 PM with Roland Mercer. They took the private booth at the end — booth seven. I brought them a bottle of the Barolo because that's what she always ordered.

It was a calm, professional meeting. They were having a business conversation. I didn't hear anything out of the ordinary. I checked on them once around 8:20 PM and everything was fine — they were talking quietly. Mr. Mercer is always a very composed, polite customer. I don't recall any argument or raised voices. No glass broke.

[AMENDED STATEMENT, following re-interview]:
I want to correct what I said before. There was an argument. I could hear it from behind the bar, which is about fifteen feet from that booth. At 8:15 PM, I heard Vivienne say something loudly — I caught the words "Seraphine" and "mine." Mr. Mercer was also speaking in a raised voice. A glass did break. I heard it hit the booth divider. I was uncomfortable and I didn't want to get involved. Mr. Mercer is a very good customer.

Danielle Reeves arrived at 9:15 PM, maybe 9:16 PM. I remember glancing at the bar clock. She and Vivienne seemed to patch things up. They left together just after 10:00 PM. They seemed fine when they left. Vivienne was laughing at something.

I was on shift until 1:00 AM. I did not leave the premises.

— Patrice Okonkwo
